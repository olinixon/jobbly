# jobbly-product-vision.md
### Jobbly — Product Vision

---

## What Is Jobbly

Jobbly is an internal operations dashboard built by and for Omniside AI. It tracks leads generated by AI voice agent campaigns from first contact through to job completion and commission reconciliation.

It is not a CRM. It is not a quoting tool. It is not a customer portal. It is a campaign operations layer — the connective tissue between an AI that generates leads, a client who owns the customer relationship, and a subcontractor who fulfills the work.

---

## The Problem It Solves

When an AI voice agent campaign generates leads at volume, three things break down quickly without a system:

**Visibility** — leads exist in call logs, spreadsheets, and message threads. Nobody has a single view of where each job is at.

**Accountability** — when a job falls through or a number doesn't match, there's no audit trail. Disputes between subcontractors and agencies are resolved by whoever has the better memory.

**Commission tracking** — without a system, calculating what's owed requires manually cross-referencing call logs, invoices, and completion confirmations. This doesn't scale past a handful of jobs.

Jobbly solves all three by being the single source of truth for every lead, every job, and every dollar.

---

## The First Campaign: Continuous Group

Jobbly is being built initially to support a guttering campaign for Continuous Group, an Auckland-based guttering company. The campaign uses an AI voice agent to call 13,000 past customers offering gutter cleaning services, starting with a 1,000 lead trial.

**The parties:**
- **Omniside AI (Oli)** — runs the campaign, owns the system, earns commission on completed jobs
- **Continuous Group** — the client, owns the customer relationship, sees campaign performance
- **Pro Water Blasting (Frank Weggen / VA)** — the subcontractor, fulfills the jobs, updates job status

**The flow:**
1. AI voice agent calls a lead
2. Lead says yes on the call
3. Lead details fire automatically into Jobbly via n8n webhook
4. Frank's VA is notified, quotes the job, and sends it to the customer under Continuous Group branding
5. Frank's VA updates job status in Jobbly as the job progresses
6. On completion, VA attaches the invoice
7. Jobbly calculates and displays commission owed to Omniside AI

---

## Success Criteria — 1,000 Lead Trial

The trial is a success when all of the following are true:

- Quotes are being sent and jobs are being completed — the campaign is generating real revenue
- Commission is hitting Oli's account with zero manual chasing or reconciliation effort
- Continuous Group can see campaign performance without asking Oli for updates
- Frank and his VA are using Jobbly independently without Oli explaining how it works
- If Frank's numbers and Oli's numbers ever differ, the dispute can be resolved in under 2 minutes by pulling up the quote number and audit log

## Longer-Term Vision

Jobbly is built for Omniside AI and will remain an internal tool — it is not being productised or sold as SaaS.

However, the architecture should be campaign-agnostic from day one. The Continuous Group guttering campaign is the first use case, but Jobbly should be capable of supporting any AI voice agent campaign regardless of industry — trades, home services, professional services, or otherwise.

This means:
- Campaign and client names should not be hardcoded into the UI or logic
- The data model should accommodate different industries and job types without structural changes
- Commission rates and markup percentages should be configurable per campaign, not hardcoded
- Multiple campaigns should be supportable in future without a rebuild

**For the MVP**, Jobbly runs one campaign (Continuous Group). The architecture just needs to not preclude adding more.

---

## Multi-Campaign Architecture

Jobbly is built campaign-aware from day one, even though the MVP launches with a single campaign (Continuous Group).

**How it works:**

Every campaign in Jobbly is a discrete entity. Leads, users, jobs, and commission data all belong to a campaign. Nothing bleeds between campaigns.

**Admin (Oli):**
After login, Oli lands on a campaign selector screen showing all active campaigns. Selecting a campaign enters that campaign's full admin view — leads, financials, users, audit log. Oli can switch between campaigns at any time. This is how Jobbly scales to multiple clients without a rebuild.

**Client and Subcontractor users:**
When Oli creates a non-admin user, they are assigned to a specific campaign at the point of creation. After login, they are automatically routed to their campaign view — no selector, no choice. They can only ever see data that belongs to their campaign.

**Campaign data isolation rules:**
- A Client View user for Continuous Group cannot see any data from any other campaign
- A Subcontractor user for Pro Water Blasting cannot see jobs from any other subcontractor or campaign
- All database queries for non-admin users are scoped by campaign ID at the API level — not just hidden in the UI

**For the MVP:**
One campaign exists (Continuous Group). The campaign selector on Oli's admin view will show one option. The architecture is in place; the campaigns just get added as new clients come on board.

---

## What Jobbly Is Not

- Not a customer-facing tool — customers never log in or interact with Jobbly
- Not a quoting engine — quote generation happens outside Jobbly (Frank's team handles this)
- Not a scheduling or dispatch tool — job logistics are handled externally
- Not a general CRM — it tracks campaign leads only, not a full customer database
- Not a billing or invoicing platform — it records and calculates, but does not process payments

---

## Design Philosophy

Jobbly is a working tool, not a showcase. Every screen should serve a clear operational purpose. The admin view should feel like a control room — dense with useful information, nothing decorative. The client view should feel like a report — clean, scannable, confidence-inspiring. The subcontractor view should feel like a job queue — simple, task-focused, impossible to misuse.

Speed of use matters more than visual polish. Oli needs to scan 50 leads in 30 seconds. Frank's VA needs to update a status in under 10 seconds. Continuous Group needs to see the headline numbers without scrolling.
